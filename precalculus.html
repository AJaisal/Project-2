Welcome to Precalculus

Precalculus is the bridge between algebra, geometry, and calculus. It deepens your understanding of functions, graphs, and mathematical patterns that model real-world behavior — from population growth to sound waves.

Core Concepts

  • Functions and Transformations: Learn how shifting, stretching, and reflecting graphs changes their behavior.
  • Exponential and Logarithmic Functions: Understand growth and decay in natural systems.
  • Trigonometric Functions: Study periodic motion and cycles in mathematics.
  • Limits: Prepare for the central ideas of calculus.
  • Conic Sections: Explore parabolas, ellipses, and hyperbolas.

Steps to Solve an Exponential Function

Exponential equations involve variables in the exponent and often describe growth or decay. Here’s a step-by-step example on how to solve them.

Example Problem:

Solve for x: 3x = 81

  1. Step 1: Write 81 as a Power of 3 81 = 34
  2. Step 2: Rewrite the Equation 3x = 34
  3. Step 3: Set Exponents Equal Since the bases are the same, x = 4.
  4. Step 4: Verify the Solution Substitute x = 4 into 3x: 34 = 81

Steps to Solve a Logarithmic Equation

Logarithms are the inverses of exponential functions. They help solve equations where the variable is an exponent.

Example Problem:

Solve for x: log2(x) = 5

  1. Step 1: Rewrite in exponential form → 25 = x
  2. Step 2: Simplify → x = 32
  3. Step 3: Check your answer → log2(32) = 5

Why Precalculus Matters

Precalculus gives you the tools to interpret mathematical relationships found in science, finance, and nature. By mastering these topics, you’ll enter calculus with the confidence to analyze limits, change, and motion with precision.

Study Tips

  • Review algebra and trigonometry regularly.
  • Use graphing calculators or Desmos to visualize functions.
  • Practice rewriting exponential and log forms.
  • Focus on understanding patterns in graphs.
