Understanding Trigonometry

Trigonometry is the study of triangles — especially the relationships between angles and sides. It plays a major role in physics, architecture, engineering, and even navigation. By mastering trigonometric ratios like sine, cosine, and tangent, you can describe patterns of motion and waves found throughout nature and technology.

Core Concepts

  • Angles and Triangles: Learn how sides and angles relate to one another.
  • Right Triangles: Use the Pythagorean theorem and trig ratios to find unknown sides.
  • The Unit Circle: Understand how sine and cosine correspond to circular motion.
  • Graphs of Trig Functions: Discover periodic patterns of sine, cosine, and tangent.

Steps to Solve a Right Triangle

When solving right triangles, we use trigonometric ratios and the Pythagorean theorem. The goal is usually to find missing sides or angles given some known information.

  1. Step 1: Identify the Known Values Look at the triangle and label the sides: opposite, adjacent, and hypotenuse relative to the given angle.
  2. Step 2: Choose the Correct Trig Ratio Decide whether to use sine, cosine, or tangent based on the sides you know:
    • sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse
    • cos(θ) = adjacent / hypotenuse
    • tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent
  3. Step 3: Substitute the Known Values Plug the given numbers into your equation. Example: sin(30°) = x / 10
  4. Step 4: Solve for the Unknown Side Multiply or divide to isolate the unknown variable. Example: x = 10 × sin(30°) → x = 5
  5. Step 5: Use the Pythagorean Theorem (if needed) If you know two sides, use a² + b² = c² to find the third.
  6. Step 6: Verify Your Answer Check calculations and ensure side ratios make sense (the hypotenuse should always be the longest side).

Quick Trig Review

  • Sine (sin) = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan) = Opposite / Adjacent

Key Formulas

  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • tanθ = sinθ / cosθ
  • c² = a² + b²
